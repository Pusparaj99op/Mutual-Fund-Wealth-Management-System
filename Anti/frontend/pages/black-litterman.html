What is the Black-Litterman Model?

The Black-Litterman Model, developed by Fischer Black and Robert Litterman at Goldman Sachs in 1990, combines market equilibrium with investor views to produce optimal portfolio allocations. It addresses key limitations of traditional Mean-Variance Optimization (MVO).

In FIMFP: We use Black-Litterman to optimize mutual fund portfolio allocations, combining market-implied returns with our AI-generated fund views to create balanced, well-diversified portfolios tailored to investor preferences.

Why Black-Litterman over Mean-Variance?

‚ùå Problems with Mean-Variance (MVO)

  • Highly sensitive to input estimates
  • Produces extreme, concentrated portfolios
  • Ignores market equilibrium
  • Unstable across time periods
  • Often suggests 100% in single asset

‚úÖ Black-Litterman Advantages

  • Stable, intuitive allocations
  • Incorporates market consensus
  • Allows expressing investor views
  • Controls confidence in views
  • Better diversified portfolios

Mathematical Foundation

Step 1: Calculate Equilibrium Returns (CAPM)

Implied Equilibrium Returns
$$\Pi = \lambda \Sigma w_{mkt}$$

Step 2: Express Investor Views

View Matrix Representation
$$P \cdot E[R] = Q + \epsilon, \quad \epsilon \sim N(0, \Omega)$$

Step 3: Combine Equilibrium with Views (Master Formula)

Black-Litterman Combined Return
$$E[R] = \left[(\tau\Sigma)^{-1} + P'\Omega^{-1}P\right]^{-1}\left[(\tau\Sigma)^{-1}\Pi + P'\Omega^{-1}Q\right]$$

Step 4: Calculate Posterior Covariance

Posterior Covariance Matrix
$$\Sigma_{BL} = \Sigma + \left[(\tau\Sigma)^{-1} + P'\Omega^{-1}P\right]^{-1}$$
