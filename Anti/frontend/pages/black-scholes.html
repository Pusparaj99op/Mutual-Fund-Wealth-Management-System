What is the Black-Scholes Model?

The Black-Scholes Model, developed by Fischer Black and Myron Scholes in 1973 (with contributions from Robert Merton), is a mathematical model for pricing European-style options. It revolutionized the derivatives market and earned Scholes and Merton the 1997 Nobel Prize in Economics.

In FIMFP: We apply Black-Scholes methodology to calculate risk premiums and Greeks (sensitivity measures) for mutual fund analysis, helping investors understand how various factors affect portfolio value.

The Black-Scholes Formula

The Black-Scholes formula for a European call option price is:

Call Option Price
$$C = S_0 N(d_1) - K e^{-rT} N(d_2)$$
Put Option Price
$$P = K e^{-rT} N(-d_2) - S_0 N(-d_1)$$

Where \(d_1\) and \(d_2\) are defined as:

$$d_1 = \frac{\ln(S_0/K) + (r + \sigma^2/2)T}{\sigma\sqrt{T}}$$

$$d_2 = d_1 - \sigma\sqrt{T}$$

The Greeks - Sensitivity Measures

The Greeks measure the sensitivity of option price to various parameters:

Delta

$$\Delta = \frac{\partial C}{\partial S} = N(d_1)$$

Rate of change of option price with respect to underlying asset price

Œì

Gamma

$$\Gamma = \frac{\partial^2 C}{\partial S^2} = \frac{N'(d_1)}{S\sigma\sqrt{T}}$$

Rate of change of Delta with respect to underlying asset price

Œò

Theta

$$\Theta = -\frac{\partial C}{\partial T}$$

Rate of change of option price with respect to time (time decay)

Vega

Vega

$$\nu = \frac{\partial C}{\partial \sigma} = S\sqrt{T}N'(d_1)$$

Rate of change of option price with respect to volatility

Rho

$$\rho = \frac{\partial C}{\partial r} = KTe^{-rT}N(d_2)$$

Rate of change of option price with respect to interest rate

Risk Premium Calculation

In the context of mutual funds, we use Black-Scholes to calculate the risk premium:

Risk Premium
$$\text{Risk Premium} = E[R_{fund}] - R_f$$

Where \(E[R_{fund}]\) is the expected return of the fund and \(R_f\) is the risk-free rate (typically 91-day T-bill rate in India, ~6-7%).
